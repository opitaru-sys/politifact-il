# Civic Press — A Design Philosophy for בדוק

## The Movement

**Civic Press** is the visual language of public verification — the editorial gravitas of a serious newspaper's fact-checking desk, distilled into screen typography. It rejects the friendly, gradient-laden, emoji-strewn aesthetic that has come to signal generic software, and instead borrows from the centuries-old craft of the press: the masthead, the stamp, the column rule, the dateline. Every choice signals: *this is checked, this is serious, this is for the public record.*

## Visual Expression

**Material** — The page is paper, not interface. A warm cream background, ink-black foreground, with a single editorial accent in deep press-red. No purple gradients, no glassmorphism, no gradient borders. Cards sit flat on the paper with hairline rules, not soft glow shadows. When something must be elevated, the lift is restrained — a single 1px border, a hint of inset.

**Color** — One sovereign accent (deep newsprint red, never bright cyan-blue) carries the brand. Verdict colors are desaturated to feel printed, not LED — a forest green, a mustard ochre, a rust red rather than emoji-bright variants. Backgrounds stay neutral so type can sing.

**Typography** — Rubik remains, but its full weight range earns its place: black for headlines that command the page, regular for editorial body, never anything in between unless purposeful. Type carries the meaning that gradients and emojis carried before. Numbers are tabular, dates restrained, labels small-caps where it earns it.

**Marks, not emojis** — The verdict badges feel like rubber stamps, not stickers. The brand wordmark replaces the generic icon-plus-text combo with a typographic logotype anchored by a single press-red dot. Sparkles, ribbon corners, halos: removed. Where an icon is genuinely needed, it is line-drawn and restrained.

**Rhythm** — Borders are hairline (1px, low-contrast). Corner radii are mixed with intent: 2px for stamps, 8px for cards, 0 for editorial blocks. Spacing follows a clear hierarchy. The composition feels labored-over: every alignment intentional, every weight choice deliberate, every gap measured. The site should look like it was cared for by someone with a strong opinion, not assembled from a prompt.

## What This Rejects

Generic SaaS blue. Friendly emojis as decoration. Gradient backgrounds. Uniform rounded corners. The "AI-app" combo of icon-tile + bold sans wordmark. Sparkle decorations. Default Tailwind palettes used unmodified.

## What This Embraces

Editorial restraint. Typographic confidence. A single, opinionated accent color. The aesthetics of public record — kshatim, mashbatim, ḥotemets. The dignity of the press.
